========================================================
  RESPONSIBLE SOCIAL MEDIA USE — COLLEGE STUDENT CHECKLIST
========================================================

A simple Do's and Don'ts guide for students like Luvish Chutani
navigating social media during college life.

--------------------------------------------------------
  SECTION 1 — DO'S (Things You SHOULD Do)
--------------------------------------------------------

  [1]  DO keep your profile professional
       Use a clear photo, a decent username, and a bio
       that reflects your academic identity. Recruiters
       and professors often look students up online.

  [2]  DO think before you post
       Ask yourself: "Would I be okay if my professor,
       parent, or future employer saw this?" If not —
       don't post it.

  [3]  DO use privacy settings on every platform
       Regularly check and update who can see your posts,
       stories, and tagged photos. You control your
       digital footprint.

  [4]  DO give credit to original creators
       Always tag or mention the source when sharing
       someone else's quote, image, or idea. Online
       plagiarism is still plagiarism.

  [5]  DO verify news before sharing
       Fact-check any article, statistic, or claim using
       reliable sources before passing it on to your
       followers or friends.

  [6]  DO take regular digital breaks
       Schedule offline hours for study, sleep, and
       real-world interaction. Constant scrolling harms
       focus, sleep, and mental well-being.

  [7]  DO engage respectfully in disagreements
       You can debate ideas and opinions online — just
       do it thoughtfully and without personal attacks.
       It reflects your maturity and character.

--------------------------------------------------------
  SECTION 2 — DON'TS (Things You Should AVOID)
--------------------------------------------------------

  [1]  DON'T share your real-time location
       Avoid live check-ins, geotags in stories, or
       posting that you're away from home. It creates
       safety and security risks.

  [2]  DON'T post confidential or sensitive information
       Never share exam papers, internal college notices,
       your Aadhaar/ID number, or financial details on
       any social platform.

  [3]  DON'T engage with or spread cyberbullying
       Don't like, share, or comment on content that
       mocks or humiliates others. Even passive
       engagement amplifies harm — and many colleges
       have policies covering online behaviour.

  [4]  DON'T post when angry or emotionally reactive
       Impulsive posts made in frustration or anger are
       the most common cause of lasting reputational
       damage for students. When in doubt, wait it out.

  [5]  DON'T trust strangers with personal details
       Be cautious of unsolicited DMs, suspicious links,
       or people asking for your phone number, address,
       or financial information online.

  [6]  DON'T replace real relationships with online ones
       Social media supplements real connections — it
       should not replace in-person friendships, family
       time, or meaningful conversations.

  [7]  DON'T rely only on your feed for news
       Algorithms show you what you already agree with.
       Seek out diverse, credible news sources to form
       balanced, well-informed opinions.

--------------------------------------------------------
  GOLDEN RULE:
  If you wouldn't say it out loud in front of your
  professor, parents, and future employer — don't
  post it.
