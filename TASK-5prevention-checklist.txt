========================================================
  8 ACTIONABLE PREVENTION TIPS FOR COLLEGE STUDENTS
========================================================

  [ TIP 1 — UPI & FINANCIAL SAFETY ]
  ------------------------------------------------------------
  NEVER enter your UPI PIN to RECEIVE money.
  A PIN is only required when you are SENDING a payment.
  If anyone asks you to enter your PIN to "accept" a payment,
  it is a fraud — reject the request immediately and block
  the contact.

  [ TIP 2 — UPI & FINANCIAL SAFETY ]
  ------------------------------------------------------------
  Do NOT scan QR codes sent by strangers online.
  Fraudsters send QR codes through WhatsApp or SMS that
  debit money from your account the moment you scan them.
  Always generate your own QR code or type the UPI ID
  manually when making or receiving payments.

  [ TIP 3 — PASSWORD HYGIENE ]
  ------------------------------------------------------------
  Use a strong, unique password for every account.
  Avoid using your name, date of birth, or "1234" as
  passwords. Use a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers,
  and special characters. Use a free password manager
  (like Bitwarden) to remember them securely.

  [ TIP 4 — TWO-FACTOR AUTHENTICATION (2FA) ]
  ------------------------------------------------------------
  Enable 2FA on all important accounts — email, Instagram,
  WhatsApp, and banking apps. Even if someone steals your
  password, they cannot log in without the second
  verification step sent to your phone or email.

  [ TIP 5 — PHISHING AWARENESS ]
  ------------------------------------------------------------
  Never click on suspicious links received via SMS, email,
  or WhatsApp — even if they appear to be from your bank,
  NPCI, Amazon, or VIT Bhopal. Fraudsters clone official
  websites pixel-for-pixel. Always type the URL directly
  into your browser and check for HTTPS and the correct
  domain name before entering any details.

  [ TIP 6 — PUBLIC WI-FI SAFETY ]
  ------------------------------------------------------------
  Avoid logging into banking apps, UPI platforms, or
  college portals on public or unsecured Wi-Fi networks
  (cafes, hostels, malls). Attackers on the same network
  can intercept your data. Use mobile data or a trusted
  VPN when accessing sensitive accounts outside your home.

  [ TIP 7 — SOCIAL MEDIA PRIVACY ]
  ------------------------------------------------------------
  Never share your Aadhaar number, PAN card, bank account
  details, college ID, or OTPs on any social media platform
  or with unknown contacts online. Legitimate institutions
  will never ask for sensitive documents or OTPs over chat
  or phone calls.

  [ TIP 8 — APP PERMISSIONS & DOWNLOADS ]
  ------------------------------------------------------------
  Only download apps from the official Google Play Store
  or Apple App Store. Never install APK files sent via
  WhatsApp or links. When installing any app, review the
  permissions it requests — a flashlight app does not need
  access to your contacts, location, or camera.

  [ TIP 9 — ONLINE SELLING SAFETY ]
  ------------------------------------------------------------
  When selling items on OLX, Facebook Marketplace, or
  Quikr, never share your UPI ID publicly in posts. Meet
  buyers in safe, public locations. Confirm that money has
  been credited to your account BEFORE handing over the
  item — do not rely on a screenshot of payment as proof.

  [ TIP 10 — CYBERBULLYING & DIGITAL HARASSMENT ]
  ------------------------------------------------------------
  If you receive threatening messages, morphed images, or
  are being harassed online, do NOT engage or retaliate.
  Take screenshots of all evidence, block the offender,
  and report the account directly on the platform. Then
  file a formal complaint at cybercrime.gov.in.

========================================================
  IF YOU ARE A VICTIM — WHERE TO REPORT
========================================================

  STEP 1 : Call the National Cyber Crime Helpline
           Helpline Number : 1930
           Available       : 24 hours, 7 days a week
           Report financial fraud within the FIRST HOUR
           for the highest chance of blocking the transaction.

  STEP 2 : File an Online Complaint
           Portal  : https://www.cybercrime.gov.in
           Managed by the Ministry of Home Affairs, Govt. of India
           You can report: UPI fraud, phishing, cyberbullying,
           identity theft, ransomware, and more.

  STEP 3 : Contact Your Bank Immediately
           Call your bank's 24x7 helpline to freeze or
           reverse a fraudulent UPI transaction as quickly
           as possible. Every minute counts.

  STEP 4 : Report to Your College Authorities
           VIT Bhopal students can also report cyber
           incidents to the IT Security Cell or Student
           Grievance Portal on the college website.

========================================================
  QUICK REFERENCE — GOLDEN RULES
========================================================

   * PIN = SEND money. Never PIN to receive.
   * OTP is secret. Never share it with anyone, ever.
   * When in doubt, don't click, don't scan, don't share.
   * Urgency is a red flag — fraudsters always rush you.
   * Report FAST: Call 1930 within the first 60 minutes.
